    Key Ideas

  1. Processor (CPU) vs RAM trade-off: A powerful CPU is great for fast processing of tasks, but without enough RAM, your system can still lag under load or multitasking. Conversely, plenty of RAM but with a weak CPU also limits performance.
  2. Budget limitations: Usually you can’t get both top CPU and lots of RAM at the same price; you have to balance what you need most.
  3. Usage purpose matters: Depending on what you do — gaming, video editing, office work, web browsing, multitasking — one component may be more important than the other.
